# SWAT User Guide

**SWAT** is the **waste-hauling** and **levy** recording system of the Surabaya City
Environmental Agency (DLH). This guide explains day-to-day use of the app — from signing
in, to monitoring fleet performance, managing master data, and recording hauling
activity.

:::tip Language
This guide is available in **Bahasa Indonesia** (primary) and **English**. Use the
language picker at the top right to switch.
:::

## Where to start

- **First time here?** Open [Getting Started → Signing In](/memulai/login), then
  [Getting to Know the Dashboard](/memulai/dashboard).
- **Want to understand access control?** See [Roles & Permissions](/memulai/peran-akses).
- **Looking for a specific feature?** Use the **search** box at the top right, or browse
  the sidebar sections:
  - **[Monitoring](/pemantauan/)** — waste tonnage, fuel consumption, hauling, levy,
    efficiency.
  - **[Master Data](/data-master/)** — vehicles, drivers, sites & routes, waste sources,
    schedule templates.
  - **[Hauling](/pengangkutan/)** — scheduling, activity recording, disposal permits,
    GPS devices.
  - **[Users & Access](/pengguna-akses/)** — users, roles, service accounts.

## Good to know

The menus you see adapt to your account's **permissions**. If a menu is missing, your
role has not been granted access to that feature — contact your administrator.

> This guide is written to follow the **running application** as the source of truth, so
> the terms and flows here match what you see on screen.
